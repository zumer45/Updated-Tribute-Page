A Tribute to Prophet Muhammad (pbuh)

Key Points in the Life of Prophet Muhammad (صلى الله عليه وسلم)

  • Born in 570 CE in Mecca, in present-day Saudi Arabia, Muhammad (صلى الله عليه وسلم) belonged to the Quraysh tribe.
  • He was orphaned at a young age, as his father, Abdullah, died before he was born, and his mother, Aminah, passed away when he was six years old.
  • His grandfather, Abdul Muttalib, and later his uncle, Abu Talib, took care of him after his parents' deaths.
  • As a young man, Muhammad (صلى الله عليه وسلم) worked as a shepherd and later as a successful merchant, gaining the nickname "Al-Amin" (the Trustworthy).
  • At the age of 25, he married Khadijah (RA), a wealthy widow who was 15 years older than him. She was his first wife, and their marriage lasted until her death 25 years later.
  • Muhammad (صلى الله عليه وسلم) would often retreat to the Cave of Hira for meditation and contemplation.
  • At the age of 40, during one of his retreats in the Cave of Hira, Muhammad (صلى الله عليه وسلم) received his first revelation from Allah through the angel Gabriel. This marked the beginning of his prophethood.
  • The revelations continued throughout his life and were compiled into the Quran, the Islamic holy book.
  • In the early years of his prophethood, Muhammad (صلى الله عليه وسلم) faced opposition and persecution from the Meccan tribes, particularly the Quraysh, who saw his teachings as a threat to their social and religious order.
  • In 622 CE, the Prophet Muhammad (صلى الله عليه وسلم) and his followers migrated from Mecca to Medina in a journey known as the Hijrah. This event marks the beginning of the Islamic calendar.
  • In Medina, Muhammad (صلى الله عليه وسلم) established the first Islamic community (ummah) and formed alliances with various tribes, creating a unified political and religious entity.
  • In 630 CE, after a series of battles and negotiations, the Prophet Muhammad (صلى الله عليه وسلم) and his followers peacefully entered and took control of Mecca, effectively uniting the Arabian Peninsula under Islam.
  • The Prophet Muhammad's (صلى الله عليه وسلم) teachings emphasized the importance of faith, prayer, charity, fasting, and pilgrimage, which form the Five Pillars of Islam.
  • The Prophet's life and sayings, known as Hadith, are considered a source of guidance and inspiration for Muslims worldwide, alongside the Quran.
  • The Prophet Muhammad (صلى الله عليه وسلم) passed away in 632 CE in Medina, leaving behind a lasting legacy and a rapidly expanding Islamic community.

Authentic Hadiths Showcasing the Beautiful Character of Prophet Muhammad (صلى الله عليه وسلم)

  • Kindness and compassion: Narrated by Aisha (RA), the Prophet Muhammad (صلى الله عليه وسلم) said: "Verily, the believers with the most complete faith are those with the best character, and the best of you are the best to their women." (Source: Sahih Tirmidhi, Book 12, Hadith 1162)
  • Humility and service to others: Narrated by Anas ibn Malik (RA), the Prophet Muhammad (صلى الله عليه وسلم) said: "None of you truly believes until he loves for his brother what he loves for himself." (Source: Sahih Bukhari, Book 2, Hadith 13)
  • Mercy and forgiveness: Narrated by Abu Huraira (RA), the Prophet Muhammad (صلى الله عليه وسلم) said: "Show mercy to those on earth, and the One in the heavens will show mercy upon you." (Source: Sunan Tirmidhi, Book 27, Hadith 1924)
  • Generosity and charity: Narrated by Ibn Abbas (RA), the Prophet Muhammad (صلى الله عليه وسلم) said: "The believer is not the one who eats when his neighbor beside him is hungry." (Source: Sunan al-Kubra, Book 36, Hadith 122)
  • The importance of good manners and conduct: Narrated by Abu Huraira (RA), the Prophet Muhammad (صلى الله عليه وسلم) said: "The most beloved of you to me and the closest to me in the Hereafter are those with the best character, and the most disliked of you to me and the farthest from me in the Hereafter are those with the worst character, the arrogant, and the boastful." (Source: Sahih Muslim, Book 47, Hadith 38)
